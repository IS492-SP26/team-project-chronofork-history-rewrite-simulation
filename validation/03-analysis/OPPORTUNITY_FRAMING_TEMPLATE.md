# Opportunity Framing

## Core Opportunity

Current AI tools can assist with historical explanation or roleplay, but they do not fully support a learner-centered, branchable, plausibility-aware counterfactual history experience.

ChronoFork addresses this opportunity by combining:
- structured decision checkpoints,
- learner intervention,
- multi-agent roleplay,
- immersive interaction,
- and reflective comparison between canonical and divergent timelines.

---

## Product Requirements Emerging from Validation

### R1. Decision-centered structure
The system should represent historical experience around explicit checkpoints rather than only free-form chat.

### R2. Branch and backtrack support
The system should let learners revisit prior decisions and compare alternative branches.

### R3. Plausibility-aware divergence
The system should distinguish:
- plausible divergence,
- low-plausibility but historically possible divergence,
- impossible or absurd divergence.

### R4. Multi-agent role consistency
The system should maintain distinct actor viewpoints, goals, and knowledge boundaries over time.

### R5. Proactive learner support
The system should reduce prompting burden by providing facilitator guidance and tips.

### R6. Reflection as a first-class component
The system should help learners compare canonical and divergent outcomes and reason about causality.

---

## Refined Concept Direction

Based on validation, our concept should emphasize:

- Observe → Intervene → Reflect
- checkpoint-based branching
- facilitator-guided engagement
- plausibility checking
- post-experience reflective analysis
